SECTION 02

The Problem

02
MARKET FRICTION • SLIDE 07

Synthetic degradation and inflated retail metrics.

The traditional garment market leverages short-staple polyester compositions that lose functional structure after three wash cycles. At the same time, intermediate distribution layers inflate the consumer cost basis by 5x-8x relative to early mill raw input value.

This results in an unsustainable loop where the user is forced into constant micro-purchasing while regional LATAM mills survive on tiny margins dictated by massive global wholesale agents.

FRICTION ZERO 01

Short Thread Poly-Blends

Break down under friction, leading to severe pilling, tactile discomfort, and micro-particles shedding.

FRICTION ZERO 02

Unjustified Middleman Markup

Broker lists, commercial leasing fees, and licensing layers double costs at three distinct points before custom shipment.

SECTION 03

The Opportunity

03
BLUE OCEAN STRATEGY • SLIDE 09

Direct Partnership with Local LATAM Producers.

By bypassing intermediaries and sourcing directly from premium Latin American makers, we offer exceptional textiles—such as super-soft long-staple pima cottons and luxury merino fibers—at honest wholesale values.

The target is to foster local manufacturing clusters while supplying an underserved, design-aware consumer class with minimalist essentials that have an average usable lifecycle index exceeding 8.5 years.
